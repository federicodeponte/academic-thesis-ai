**Location:** Entire "Einleitung" section (1926 words)
**Problem:** The introduction is exceptionally long for a Master's thesis. The current "Einleitung" duplicates content that is explicitly planned for "Kapitel 2: Literatur칲bersicht." **Evidence:** The "Hintergrund zum CO2-Zertifikatehandel" section (paragraphs 4-6) is essentially a mini-literature review.
**Fix:** Drastically condense the "Einleitung." Focus on introducing the overarching problem, briefly stating the relevance of EHS, highlighting the core research gap, and then presenting the research questions and thesis structure. Move all detailed historical context, specific system descriptions (like EU ETS phases), and in-depth discussions of theoretical advantages/disadvantages to "Kapitel 2: Literatur칲bersicht." Aim for an introduction of 500-800 words maximum.

### Issue 2: Overclaim of "Causal Links"
**Location:** Forschungsziele, Paragraph 11
**Claim:** "Insbesondere soll die Arbeit die kausalen Zusammenh칛nge zwischen der Implementierung und Weiterentwicklung von EHS und den beobachteten Emissionsreduktionen aufzeigen."
**Problem:** Establishing robust *causal* links in complex socio-economic systems like EHS is methodologically extremely challenging, often requiring sophisticated econometric techniques or quasi-experimental designs that may be difficult to execute comprehensively within the scope of a Master's thesis. Without a detailed methodology, this strong claim appears to be an overstatement of what can realistically be achieved. A Master's thesis typically aims to identify *correlations*, *associations*, or *contributions*, rather than definitive causality.
**Evidence:** The claim is made without any preceding discussion of the specific, highly rigorous methodology required to support it.
**Fix:** Rephrase this claim to be more realistic. Instead of "kausale Zusammenh칛nge aufzeigen," consider phrases like "Zusammenh칛nge untersuchen," "Beitr칛ge analysieren," "Einflussfaktoren identifizieren," or "Evidenz f칲r die Wirkung bewerten." Ensure the language aligns with the feasible methodological rigor.
**Severity:** 游댮 High - creates an expectation that the thesis might not be able to meet, potentially leading to disappointment in the results section.

### Issue 3: Ambitious Scope of Research Question 3
**Location:** Forschungsziele, Research Question 3
**Claim:** "Welche Rolle spielen der CO2-Preis und die damit verbundenen Investitionsanreize f칲r die technologische Innovation und den 칖bergang zu kohlenstoffarmen Technologien innerhalb der EHS-Sektoren?"
**Problem:** This question is extremely broad and complex. Measuring "technological innovation" and definitively attributing its development and adoption to specific CO2 price incentives within the EHS framework is a massive undertaking. It involves intricate data collection, advanced econometric modeling, and often qualitative analysis of innovation processes. This might be too ambitious for a Master's thesis, risking a superficial treatment of a critical but difficult-to-measure area.
**Evidence:** The question itself covers multiple complex concepts: CO2 price, investment incentives, technological innovation, and transition to low-carbon technologies.
**Fix:** Either narrow the scope of this question significantly (e.g., focus on a specific type of innovation, a specific technology, or a specific sector) or rephrase it to be more exploratory and less definitive (e.g., "Wie *k칬nnten* sich CO2-Preise auf technologische Innovationen auswirken..."). Consider if this question could be split or if a more focused approach is necessary.
**Severity:** 游댮 High - threatens the feasibility and depth of the research, potentially leading to an incomplete or overly general analysis.

## MODERATE ISSUES (Should Address)

### Issue 4: Broadness of "Kernfrage" in Problemstellung
**Location:** Problemstellung, Paragraph 7
**Claim:** The "Kernfrage" includes whether EHS leads to reductions "auf eine sozial gerechte und wirtschaftlich effiziente Weise."
**Problem:** While social justice and economic efficiency are crucial aspects of climate policy, integrating a comprehensive analysis of these alongside emissions reduction might be too broad for a single Master's thesis. Each of these aspects (emissions reduction, social justice, economic efficiency) could be a thesis in itself. Trying to cover all three risks diluting the focus and depth of analysis on the primary goal (emissions reduction).
**Fix:** Consider if the primary focus should be narrowed to emissions reduction, with social justice and economic efficiency mentioned as important contextual factors or limitations that the thesis *touches upon* rather than fully answers. If these are central, ensure your RQs and methodology explicitly address how you will measure and analyze these aspects.

### Issue 5: Aspirational Language in Research Question 4
**Location:** Forschungsziele, Research Question 4
**Claim:** "...um deren Klimaschutzwirkung zu maximieren?"
**Problem:** "Maximieren" is a very strong and ambitious goal. A Master's thesis is more likely to provide insights for "optimizing," "improving," or "enhancing" climate protection effectiveness, rather than achieving its maximum potential.
**Fix:** Replace "maximieren" with a more realistic term like "optimieren," "verbessern," or "steigern."

### Issue 6: Lack of Explicitly Addressed Counterarguments/Balanced View for EHS
**Location:** Problemstellung (Paragraphs 7-10)
**Problem:** While the "Problemstellung" effectively details the *critiques* and *weaknesses* of EHS (over-allocation, carbon leakage, distributional effects), it could benefit from a more explicit acknowledgment of the *theoretical strengths* or *intended benefits* that make EHS a preferred instrument despite these challenges. Although mentioned briefly earlier as "vielversprechende Ans칛tze," a more balanced summary of the arguments *for* EHS (e.g., cost-effectiveness, flexibility, innovation incentives) before diving into the criticisms would strengthen the rationale for studying EHS in detail.
**Fix:** Briefly reiterate or elaborate on the core theoretical advantages of EHS as a policy instrument within the problem statement, perhaps as a counterpoint to the critiques, before concluding with the need for further empirical investigation due to mixed results and persistent challenges.

### Issue 7: Generalizability of "Forschungsziele"
**Location:** Forschungsziele, RQs 1 & 2
**Problem:** RQ1 focuses specifically on the EU ETS, while RQ2 refers to "Emissionshandelssystemen" in plural. This creates a slight ambiguity regarding the scope. Will the thesis focus exclusively on the EU ETS, or will it draw comparisons with other EHS?
**Fix:** Clarify the scope of the RQs. If the thesis primarily focuses on the EU ETS, ensure the RQs reflect this consistently. If other EHS are considered, briefly mention which ones or how they will be integrated into the analysis.
