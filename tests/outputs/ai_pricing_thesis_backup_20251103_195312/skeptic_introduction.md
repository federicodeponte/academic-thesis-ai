## MAJOR ISSUES (Must Address)

### Issue 1: Overclaim - "Novel Conceptual Framework"
**Location:** Research Objective 2, and implicitly throughout the problem statement leading to it.
**Claim:** "To develop a novel conceptual framework for pricing agentic AI services..."
**Problem:** While the paper *aims* to develop a novel framework, stating it as "novel" in the objective (and thus in the introduction) is an overclaim until the framework itself is presented and its novelty rigorously demonstrated (e.g., by comparing it to existing frameworks and highlighting unique contributions). The introduction sets a very high bar without providing initial evidence of this novelty.
**Evidence:** The introduction outlines the *need* for a framework but doesn't offer any preliminary insights into *why* it will be novel beyond integrating principles from existing fields.
**Fix:** Rephrase to "To develop a conceptual framework for pricing agentic AI services that accounts for their unique characteristics, integrating principles from..." or "To propose a novel conceptual framework..." and then clearly demonstrate novelty in Section 4.
**Severity:** 游댮 High - affects the fundamental claim of the paper's contribution.

## MODERATE ISSUES (Should Address)

### Issue 2: Uncited Strong Claim
**Location:** Paragraph 2, sentence 6
**Claim:** "The proliferation of these systems marks a paradigm shift, moving beyond mere data processing to intelligent action execution, which promises to redefine productivity and innovation."
**Problem:** "Paradigm shift" is a very strong claim. While plausible in the context of AI, it should ideally be supported by a citation from a reputable source that discusses this shift, or be hedged slightly (e.g., "appears to mark a paradigm shift").
**Missing:** A supporting citation for the "paradigm shift" claim.
**Fix:** Add a citation to a paper/report discussing this shift, or rephrase to a slightly more cautious statement.

### Issue 3: Potential Weakness in Evaluation Scope
**Location:** Research Objective 3
**Claim:** "To evaluate the applicability and implications of the proposed framework through a series of theoretical case studies..."
**Problem:** While theoretical case studies are valuable, using them to "evaluate applicability" can be perceived as a limitation. "Applicability" often implies real-world or empirical validation. Relying solely on theoretical cases might limit the perceived strength of the evaluation.
**Missing:** Acknowledgment of this limitation or justification for the theoretical approach.
**Fix:** Either add a brief justification for using theoretical case studies (e.g., "given the nascent market..." or "as a foundational step...") or acknowledge this as a limitation in the discussion section. For the introduction, perhaps rephrase "evaluate the applicability" to "illustrate the applicability" or "explore the potential applicability."

### Issue 4: Implied but Unexplained Connection to LLMs
**Location:** Paragraph 2, sentence 1-3
**Observation:** The introduction mentions LLMs and foundation models "paving the way for a new generation of sophisticated AI systems" and then immediately transitions to agentic AI.
**Problem:** While LLMs are often components of agentic AI, the exact nature of this "paving the way" or the relationship between the two could be slightly clearer for readers less familiar with the nuances. The connection is implied but not explicitly defined beyond a general statement.
**Missing:** A concise sentence explaining *how* LLMs/foundation models specifically enable or contribute to the capabilities of agentic AI (e.g., "by providing advanced reasoning and generation capabilities essential for autonomous agents").
**Fix:** Add a brief clarifying sentence to strengthen the logical link.

## Logical Gaps

### Gap 1: Implicit Assumption of "Value" Definition
**Location:** Paragraph 3, sentence 5 (and throughout the problem statement)
**Logic:** The paper strongly argues for aligning price with "actual business value generated" rather than cost/complexity.
**Missing:** While the problem is clear, the introduction doesn't explicitly state that defining and quantifying this "value" is itself a significant part of the challenge the framework will address. It's implied, but making it explicit would strengthen the problem definition.
**Fix:** Add a sentence clarifying that a core aspect of the challenge is the *difficulty in quantifying* the "actual business value" of agentic AI, and how the proposed framework will help address this.

## Methodological Concerns (Implicit from Objectives)

### Concern 1: Scope of "Review and Categorize"
**Issue:** Objective 1 states "To systematically review and categorize existing pricing models applied to AI services."
**Risk:** "AI services" is a broad term. While the focus is agentic AI, the review might inadvertently become too broad or too narrow if not carefully scoped.
**Reviewer Question:** "Will the review explicitly filter for models relevant to *agentic* AI, or will it cover all AI services and then discuss limitations for agents?"
**Suggestion:** Clarify the scope of the review within the objective itself (e.g., "...applied to AI services, *with a specific focus on their applicability to agentic AI capabilities*...").
