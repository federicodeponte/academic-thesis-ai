## Core Argument Flow

**Thesis Statement:** While Open Source Software holds immense potential to drive global positive change and "save the world" through collaboration and innovation, its full impact is contingent upon the long-term sustainability of its projects, which current frameworks inadequately address, particularly in developing contexts and for emerging technologies; therefore, a novel, holistic sustainability framework is necessary to guide sustainable OSS development and maximize its global impact.

**Logical Progression:**
1.  OSS presents a powerful paradigm for solving global problems (Introduction).
2.  However, the long-term viability and impact of OSS projects are often hindered by complex sustainability challenges (Introduction).
3.  Existing research and frameworks on OSS sustainability are fragmented, lack comprehensive empirical validation, and overlook critical contextual factors specific to developing countries and emerging technological domains like AI/ML and supply chain security (Literature Review, Gap).
4.  We propose a novel, holistic Open Source Software Sustainability Framework (OSSF) that integrates technical, economic, social, security, ethical, and contextual dimensions (Theoretical Framework).
5.  Through illustrative case studies, we demonstrate how applying the OSSF can help assess, enhance, and ensure the sustainable development and impactful deployment of OSS solutions in diverse global contexts (Case Studies).
6.  This OSSF provides a structured approach for practitioners and policymakers to foster sustainable OSS, thereby unlocking its potential for significant global impact and addressing critical societal challenges (Discussion).

    -   **Indicators:** [TODO: VERIFY] Existence of ethical guidelines/principles, documented bias audits, data governance policies, transparency reports, user consent mechanisms, impact assessments.

Write in this order:
1.  **Theoretical Framework:** Define the OSSF clearly. This is the core contribution.
2.  **Case Studies:** Apply the defined OSSF to your chosen projects. This directly uses Section 3.
3.  **Literature Review:** Now you know exactly what gaps your OSSF fills and what prior work it builds upon/extends.
4.  **Introduction:** Frame the problem, research questions, and contributions based on the now-solidified framework and literature review.
5.  **Discussion:** Interpret the case study findings in light of the OSSF and literature.
6.  **Conclusion:** Summarize the entire argument and contributions.
7.  **Abstract & Title:** Last – summarizes everything concisely once the full paper is complete.

## Quality Checks

Each section should answer:
-   **Introduction:** Why should I care about OSS sustainability and its global impact? What critical problem does this paper address?
-   **Literature Review:** What do we already know about OSS sustainability, and crucially, what are the significant gaps that our paper fills?
-   **Theoretical Framework:** What is our proposed holistic OSSF, how is it structured, and what are its core components?
-   **Case Studies:** How does the OSSF work in practice? What insights does it provide when applied to real-world OSS projects?
-   **Discussion:** What do our framework and its application mean for the field? How does it advance theory and practice, and what are its limitations?
-   **Conclusion:** Why does our OSSF matter? What are its key contributions, and what's next?

-   **Know:** Basic concepts of Open Source Software, its general benefits, and general academic writing conventions. They may be familiar with some individual aspects of software sustainability.

-   **Emphasize:** The novelty and comprehensiveness of the OSSF, its direct relevance to the identified gaps, and its strong practical implications for enhancing the global impact of OSS. Highlight how it helps "save the world" by making OSS truly sustainable.
