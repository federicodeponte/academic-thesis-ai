## Formatting Requirements

### Manuscript Specifications
- **Font:** Times New Roman 12pt
- **Line Spacing:** Double
- **Margins:** 1 inch all sides
- **Page Numbers:** Top right
- **Headings:** Numbered

### Section Heading Levels (APA 7th Edition Style)
- **Level 1:** Centered, Bold, Title Case (e.g., **1. Introduction**)
- **Level 2:** Left-aligned, Bold, Title Case (e.g., **1.1 Background and Motivation**)
- **Level 3:** Left-aligned, Bold, Italic, Title Case (e.g., ***1.1.1 Historical Context***)
- **Level 4:** Indented, Bold, Sentence case, Ending with a period. (e.g., **1.1.1.1 Early developments.**)
- **Level 5:** Indented, Bold, Italic, Sentence case, Ending with a period. (e.g., ***1.1.1.1.1 Key figures.***)

### Citation Format
- **In-text:** (Author, Year) or Author (Year)
- **Bibliography:** Full APA 7th Edition format specification (see below).

## Formatted Structure

### Title
**Format:** Bold, Centered, 14pt (First page only, Level 1 heading style for subsequent pages if needed for running head)
**Max Length:** 100 characters (including spaces)
**Suggested:** **The Transformative Power of Open Source: A Framework for Global Impact in the Age of AI and Decentralization**

### Author Information
**Format:**
- Name(s): Centered, below title
- Affiliation(s): Centered, below name(s)
- Email(s): Centered, below affiliation(s)
- ORCID: Optional, Centered, below email(s)

### Abstract
**Heading:** **Abstract** (Bold, Centered, Level 1 heading, no section number)
**Length:** 250-300 words
**Structure:**
- Background (2 sentences): Global challenges are increasingly complex, demanding novel, collaborative solutions. Open Source Software (OSS) offers a powerful paradigm for addressing these.
- Objective (1 sentence): This paper proposes a framework detailing specific mechanisms through which modern OSS, integrating emerging technologies like AI and blockchain, can drive transformative global impact.
- Methods (2-3 sentences): We synthesize existing literature on OSS, innovation, and societal impact to develop a theoretical framework. This framework is then illustrated through carefully selected case studies demonstrating OSS's role in addressing complex global problems.
- Results (2-3 sentences): The case studies exemplify how OSS fosters decentralized collaboration, rapid innovation, and accessible solutions across technological, social, and economic dimensions, particularly when leveraging contemporary advancements.
- Conclusions (1-2 sentences): The paper advances our understanding of OSS's societal value, offering a strategic roadmap for harnessing its potential to tackle critical global challenges.

**Keywords:** 3-6 keywords (e.g., Open Source Software, Global Challenges, AI, Blockchain, Social Impact, Collaboration)

## Style Guide

### Academic Tone
-   ✅ **Use:** "The results indicate...", "We observed...", "This suggests..."
-   ❌ **Avoid:** "Obviously...", "Clearly...", "It's interesting that..."

### Tense Usage
-   **Introduction:** Present tense (current state of knowledge, challenges)
-   **Literature Review:** Past tense (what others found), Present tense (what the literature currently shows)
-   **Theoretical Framework:** Present tense (describing the proposed framework)
-   **Case Studies and Analysis:** Past tense (what was observed in the cases)
-   **Discussion:** Present tense (what the findings mean, implications)
-   **Conclusion:** Present tense (summary of contributions, final thoughts)

### Voice
-   **Active vs Passive:** Prefer active for clarity, passive for objectivity. Strive for a balance.
-   ✅ "We analyzed the data" (active, clear)
-   ✅ "The data were analyzed" (passive, objective)
