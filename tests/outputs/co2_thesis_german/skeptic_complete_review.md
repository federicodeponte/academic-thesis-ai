## MAJOR ISSUES (Must Address)

### Issue 1: Overly Ambitious Research Questions for Thesis Scope
**Location:** Section "Vor diesem Hintergrund verfolgt..." (Research Questions)
**Claim:** The set of RQs, especially RQ2 and RQ4, implies a scope suitable for a larger research project (e.g., PhD dissertation or a multi-researcher study) rather than a Master's thesis.
**Problem:**
    *   **RQ2 (Kausale Wirkung):** Empirically establishing the *causal effect* of EU-EHS on GHG reductions, while isolating it from other factors, is methodologically extremely challenging and requires significant data, advanced econometrics, and often multiple studies. Claiming to provide *the* empirical evidence for causal impact might be an overstatement of what a single Master's thesis can definitively achieve.
    *   **RQ4 (Interaktion externe Faktoren):** Analyzing the *interaction* between EU-EHS mechanisms and *multiple* external factors (economy, energy prices, tech innovations) on emissions development is also a very complex task, requiring sophisticated modeling and extensive data, potentially exceeding the time and resource constraints of a Master's thesis.
**Evidence:** The phrasing "umfassend zu analysieren und zu bewerten" (overall goal) combined with these broad RQs.
**Fix:**
    *   **Refine RQs:** Narrow the scope of RQ2 and RQ4. Instead of definitively proving *causal effects*, perhaps focus on *identifying strong correlations*, *analyzing existing empirical evidence*, or *evaluating specific methodological approaches* to causal inference. For RQ4, focus on a *subset* of external factors or a specific type of interaction.
    *   **Acknowledge Scope Limitations:** Explicitly state that the thesis will *contribute* to understanding these complex issues rather than providing a definitive, comprehensive answer.
**Severity:** üî¥ High - affects the feasibility and credibility of the entire thesis.
