## Formatting Requirements

### Manuscript Specifications (APA 7th Edition)
-   **Font:** Times New Roman 12pt (or Arial 11pt, Calibri 11pt, Georgia 11pt, Lato 11pt)
-   **Line Spacing:** Double
-   **Margins:** 1 inch all sides
-   **Page Numbers:** Top right, starting from the Title Page (page 1)
-   **Headings:** Numbered for clarity and ease of navigation in a complex theoretical paper.

### Section Heading Levels (APA 7th Edition)
-   **Level 1:** Centered, Bold, Title Case (e.g., **1. Introduction**)
-   **Level 2:** Left-Aligned, Bold, Title Case (e.g., **1.1 Background and Motivation**)
-   **Level 3:** Left-Aligned, Bold, Italic, Title Case (e.g., ***1.1.1 Historical Context***)
-   **Level 4:** Indented, Bold, Title Case, ending with a period. (e.g., **1.1.1.1 Early developments.**)
-   **Level 5:** Indented, Bold, Italic, Title Case, ending with a period. (e.g., ***1.1.1.1.1 Key open source projects.***)

### Citation Format (APA 7th Edition)
-   **In-text:** (Author, Year) or Author (Year)
-   **Bibliography:** Full format specification for various source types (journal articles, books, websites, etc.) following APA 7th guidelines.

## Style Guide

### Academic Tone
-   ✅ **Use:** "The results indicate...", "We observed...", "This suggests...", "The framework elucidates..."
-   ❌ **Avoid:** "Obviously...", "Clearly...", "It's interesting that...", "I think..."

### Tense Usage
-   **Introduction:** Present tense (current state, problem)
-   **Literature Review:** Past tense (what others found), Present tense (current understanding, gaps)
-   **Theoretical Framework:** Present tense (describing the proposed framework)
-   **Methodology (Case Studies):** Past tense (what was done to select/analyze cases)
-   **Case Studies:** Past tense (what was found/observed in the cases)
-   **Discussion:** Present tense (what the findings mean, implications)
-   **Conclusion:** Present tense (summary of contributions, future outlook)

### Voice
-   **Active vs Passive:** Prefer active for clarity, passive for objectivity. Aim for a balance.
-   ✅ "We analyzed the data" (active, clear)
-   ✅ "The data were analyzed" (passive, objective)
