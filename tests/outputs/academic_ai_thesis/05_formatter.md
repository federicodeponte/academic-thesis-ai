## Style Guide

### Academic Tone
- ✅ **Use:** "The framework posits...", "This analysis suggests...", "We argue that..."
- ❌ **Avoid:** "Obviously...", "Clearly...", "It's interesting that..."

### Tense Usage
- **Introduction:** Present tense (current state of knowledge, the paper's aim)
- **Literature Review:** Past tense (what others found), Present tense (critique of current knowledge)
- **Theoretical Framework:** Present tense (describing the framework, its components, and proposed interactions)
- **Illustrative Case Studies:** Present tense (describing how the framework *would* apply) or Future tense (projected outcomes)
- **Discussion:** Present tense (what the framework means, implications)

### Voice
- **Active vs Passive:** Prefer active for clarity, passive for objectivity where appropriate.
- ✅ "We propose a novel framework" (active, clear)
- ✅ "The framework is designed to..." (passive, objective)
