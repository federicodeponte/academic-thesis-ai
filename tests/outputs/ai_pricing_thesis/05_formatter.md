## Style Guide

### Academic Tone
-   ✅ **Use:** "The results indicate...", "We observed...", "This suggests..."
-   ❌ **Avoid:** "Obviously...", "Clearly...", "It's interesting that..."

### Tense Usage
-   **Introduction:** Present tense (current state, problem statement)
-   **Literature Review:** Past tense (what others found, historical context)
-   **Proposed Framework:** Present tense (describing the framework, its components)
-   **Case Studies:** Past tense (what was applied, what was demonstrated)
-   **Discussion:** Present tense (what it means, implications, interpretations)
-   **Conclusion:** Present tense (summary of contributions, future outlook)

### Voice
-   **Active vs Passive:** Prefer active for clarity, passive for objectivity. Balance for readability and academic convention.
-   ✅ "We analyzed the data" (active, clear)
-   ✅ "The data were analyzed" (passive, objective)
