## MAJOR ISSUES (Must Address)

### Issue 1: Overly Ambitious Research Questions for Thesis Scope
**Location:** Section "Vor diesem Hintergrund verfolgt..." (Research Questions)
**Claim:** The set of RQs, especially RQ2 and RQ4, implies a scope suitable for a larger research project (e.g., PhD dissertation or a multi-researcher study) rather than a Master's thesis.
**Problem:**
    *   **RQ2 (Kausale Wirkung):** Empirically establishing the *causal effect* of EU-EHS on GHG reductions, while isolating it from other factors, is methodologically extremely challenging and requires significant data, advanced econometrics, and often multiple studies. Claiming to provide *the* empirical evidence for causal impact might be an overstatement of what a single Master's thesis can definitively achieve.
    *   **RQ4 (Interaktion externe Faktoren):** Analyzing the *interaction* between EU-EHS mechanisms and *multiple* external factors (economy, energy prices, tech innovations) on emissions development is also a very complex task, requiring sophisticated modeling and extensive data, potentially exceeding the time and resource constraints of a Master's thesis.
**Evidence:** The phrasing "umfassend zu analysieren und zu bewerten" (overall goal) combined with these broad RQs.
**Fix:**
    *   **Refine RQs:** Narrow the scope of RQ2 and RQ4. Instead of definitively proving *causal effects*, perhaps focus on *identifying strong correlations*, *analyzing existing empirical evidence*, or *evaluating specific methodological approaches* to causal inference. For RQ4, focus on a *subset* of external factors or a specific type of interaction.
    *   **Acknowledge Scope Limitations:** Explicitly state that the thesis will *contribute* to understanding these complex issues rather than providing a definitive, comprehensive answer.
**Severity:** üî¥ High - affects the feasibility and credibility of the entire thesis.

### Issue 2: Vague "Research Gap" Identification
**Location:** Problemstellung (para 5), and Chapter 2 outline.
**Claim:** "identifiziert bestehende Forschungsl√ºcken, die durch die vorliegende Arbeit geschlossen werden sollen." (Chapter 2 description)
**Problem:** While the introduction successfully highlights the "Ambivalenz" and "kontroverser Diskussionen," it doesn't clearly articulate *what specific gap* in this debate the thesis aims to fill. Simply stating that "the debate is complex" is not a research gap. A gap could be:

    *   A new methodological approach not yet applied to EU-EHS.

**Fix:** In the problem statement, after discussing the "Ambivalenz," clearly state: "Despite this extensive research, there remains a specific gap concerning [e.g., the long-term causal impact in newly covered sectors, a comparative analysis using a specific novel methodology, the integrated assessment of specific interactions X and Y], which this thesis aims to address." ### Issue 3: Potential for Circular Reasoning in Problem Statement
**Location:** Problemstellung (para 5)
**Claim:** "Die zentrale Problemstellung dieser Masterarbeit liegt in der Ambivalenz der bisherigen Forschungsergebnisse und √∂ffentlichen Diskussionen bez√ºglich der tats√§chlichen Klimaschutzwirkung des EU-EHS."
**Problem:** This phrasing risks defining the problem as the *existence of a debate* rather than a substantive scientific question *within* that debate. If the goal is merely to *describe* the ambivalence, it might not constitute a novel research contribution.
**Missing:** A deeper dive into *why* this ambivalence persists, beyond mere "complexity." Are there specific methodological disagreements, differing assumptions, or data limitations that contribute to the conflicting findings? Identifying these would strengthen the problem statement.
**Fix:** Rephrase to focus on resolving or shedding new light on *aspects* of this ambivalence. For example, "This thesis addresses the persistent ambivalence by *critically evaluating the methodologies employed by conflicting studies* (if a meta-analysis), or by *providing new empirical evidence for X under conditions Y* (if primary research)."
**Severity:** üî¥ High - impacts the perceived novelty and depth of the research.

### Issue 4: "Robust and Evidence-Based Analysis" as an Overclaim
**Location:** End of Research Questions section.
**Claim:** "Die Arbeit wird dabei sowohl qualitative als auch quantitative Forschungsmethoden nutzen, um eine robuste und evidenzbasierte Analyse zu gew√§hrleisten."
**Problem:** While an admirable goal, "robust" and "evidenzbasiert" are strong claims, especially when dealing with complex causal inference in a Master's thesis. Achieving true robustness often involves sensitivity analyses, multiple model specifications, and extensive data, which might be difficult to fully execute.
**Fix:** Hedge this statement. For example, "Die Arbeit wird dabei sowohl qualitative als auch quantitative Forschungsmethoden nutzen, um eine *fundierte* Analyse zu erm√∂glichen" or "um eine *m√∂glichst robuste* Analyse zu gew√§hrleisten." Alternatively, specify *how* robustness will be ensured (e.g., "durch die Anwendung von X und Y").
**Severity:** üü° Moderate - important for academic integrity, but less critical than scope issues.

## MODERATE ISSUES (Should Address)

### Issue 5: "Bevorzugter Ansatz" Claim
**Location:** Paragraph 2
**Claim:** "Innerhalb der CO2-Bepreisungssysteme haben sich Emissionshandelssysteme (EHS)... als ein bevorzugter Ansatz etabliert, insbesondere in der Europ√§ischen Union."
**Problem:** While EHS is prominent, "bevorzugter Ansatz" can be subjective. Many economists and policymakers still advocate for carbon taxes (Pigouvian taxes) due to their price certainty and administrative simplicity, even if EHS is currently more widely adopted in some regions. The statement might oversimplify the ongoing debate.
**Fix:** Rephrase to "ein weit verbreiteter und wichtiger Ansatz" or "ein in vielen Regionen, insbesondere in der Europ√§ischen Union, implementierter Ansatz." Acknowledge the debate briefly if space allows.
**Severity:** üü° Moderate - minor nuance, but important for accuracy in academic writing.

### Issue 6: "Minimieren der Gesamtkosten" - Theoretical vs. Practical
**Location:** Paragraph 3
**Claim:** "Dieser Mechanismus soll sicherstellen, dass Emissionsminderungen dort erfolgen, wo sie am kosteng√ºnstigsten sind, und somit die Gesamtkosten des Klimaschutzes f√ºr die Wirtschaft minimieren."
**Problem:** This describes the *ideal theoretical outcome* of an EHS. In practice, market imperfections, transaction costs, political interventions, and information asymmetries can prevent the system from truly minimizing total costs. The "soll sicherstellen" (is intended to ensure) is good, but the "minimieren" might be too strong without qualification.
**Fix:** Add a hedge, e.g., "...und somit die Gesamtkosten des Klimaschutzes f√ºr die Wirtschaft *theoretisch* minimieren *soll*," or "...und somit *effizient* zur Minimierung der Gesamtkosten beitragen kann."
**Severity:** üü° Moderate - important for distinguishing theory from practice.

## Methodological Concerns (from Introduction's hints)

### Concern 1: Causality Claim vs. Methodological Detail
**Issue:** The RQs and Chapter 3 description heavily emphasize "kausale Wirkung" (causal effect) and "Isolierung der kausalen Wirkung."
**Risk:** This sets a very high bar. Without more explicit detail in the introduction about the *types* of causal inference methods (e.g., Difference-in-Differences, Synthetic Control, Regression Discontinuity) that will be employed to *credibly* isolate these effects, the claim appears ambitious.
**Reviewer Question:** "How will the thesis credibly isolate causal effects given the multitude of confounding factors in real-world policy implementation?"
**Suggestion:** While Chapter 3 will detail this, the introduction could briefly hint at the *approach* to causal inference (e.g., "Diese Arbeit wird sich auf quasi-experimentelle Ans√§tze st√ºtzen, um die Herausforderungen der Kausalit√§tsanalyse zu adressieren...").

### Concern 2: Balance of Qualitative and Quantitative Methods
**Issue:** The introduction states both qualitative and quantitative methods will be used, but only quantitative methods are hinted at with specifics (econometrics, statistics).
**Risk:** Appears as if qualitative methods are an afterthought or not central to answering the RQs.
**Question:** "What specific qualitative methods will be used, and how will they complement the quantitative analysis to answer the stated research questions?"
**Fix:** As mentioned in Moderate Issue 10, provide more detail or examples for qualitative methods.
