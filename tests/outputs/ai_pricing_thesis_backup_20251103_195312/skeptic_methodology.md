## MAJOR ISSUES (Must Address)

### Issue 1: Missing Foundational Case Study Citations
**Location:** "Case Study Selection" section, including "Rationale for Case-Based Approach."
**Claim:** The use and rationale of case study methodology.
**Problem:** The section explicitly references foundational texts (Yin, Eisenhardt, Stake) but marks them as `cite_MISSING`. These are critical for establishing the academic rigor and accepted practices of case study research.
**Evidence:** `cite_MISSING: Yin, 2018, Case Study Research`, `cite_MISSING: Eisenhardt, 1989, Building Theories from Case Study Research`, `cite_MISSING: Stake, 1995, The Art of Case Study Research`.
**Fix:** Add the full, correct citations for these foundational works to the reference list and ensure they are properly linked in the text. This is non-negotiable for a methodology section.

### Issue 2: Ambiguous "Theoretical Analysis" Component
**Location:** Introduction, "Framework for Comparing AI Agent Pricing Models" introduction, "Synthesis and Theory Building."
**Claim:** The study employs a "theoretical analysis, augmented by a comparative case study approach."
**Problem:** While the framework development is clear, the *process* of "theoretical analysis" itself, as a distinct component from the framework or the case study analysis, is not explicitly defined or detailed. It's unclear how theory is analyzed *prior to* or *in conjunction with* the case studies, beyond the creation of the framework.
**Missing:** A clear description of what "theoretical analysis" entails as a methodology. Is it a literature review leading to propositions? A conceptual modeling exercise? How does it interact with the empirical case study work?
**Fix:** Explicitly define and elaborate on the "theoretical analysis" methodology. Clarify its scope, methods (e.g., systematic literature review, conceptual synthesis), and how its findings inform or are integrated with the case study component.
**Severity:** ðŸ”´ High - a core methodological claim is vague and underspecified.

### Issue 3: Limitations of Data Collection (Public & Inferred)
**Location:** "Data Collection and Analysis" section.
**Claim:** Data was "primarily collected from publicly available sources" and "information on cost drivers and value propositions was inferred."
**Problem:** Reliance solely on public data (websites, marketing materials) and inferences can introduce significant limitations regarding depth, accuracy, and potential bias. Proprietary information, actual internal cost structures, and true strategic rationales are often not publicly disclosed.
**Missing:** An explicit acknowledgment of these limitations, a discussion of potential biases, and how they will be mitigated or factored into the interpretation of findings.
**Fix:** Add a dedicated paragraph or section under "Data Collection and Analysis" to discuss the inherent limitations of using publicly available and inferred data. Explain how the researchers plan to address or acknowledge these limitations when drawing conclusions.
**Severity:** ðŸ”´ High - impacts the validity and generalizability of findings.

## Methodological Concerns

### Concern 1: Generalizability of Case Study Findings
**Issue:** While the case study selection criteria aim for diversity, case studies inherently provide in-depth, contextualized insights that may not be directly generalizable to the broader, rapidly evolving landscape of AI agents and their pricing.
**Risk:** Over-generalization of findings from a limited set of cases.
**Reviewer Question:** "How do you plan to address the generalizability limitations inherent in a case study methodology, especially given the rapid evolution of AI agent markets?"
**Suggestion:** Add a section (perhaps in the Discussion or a dedicated "Limitations" section) to explicitly discuss the generalizability of the findings and the specific measures taken (e.g., diverse selection) to mitigate this limitation.

### Concern 2: Researcher Bias in Qualitative Inference
**Issue:** The methodology involves "inferring" information from publicly available data and conducting a "qualitative comparative analysis." These processes are susceptible to researcher bias in interpretation and coding.
**Risk:** Subjective interpretation influencing the findings.
**Reviewer Question:** "What measures were taken to ensure objectivity and minimize researcher bias during the data inference, extraction, and qualitative analysis stages?"
**Suggestion:** Briefly describe steps taken to enhance objectivity, such as structured coding protocols, cross-referencing information, or (if applicable) involvement of multiple coders to ensure inter-rater reliability.
